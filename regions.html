About Eastern Region

The Eastern Region of Ghana, nestled in the southeastern part of the country, offers a blend of cultural heritage, natural beauty, and adventure. From its vibrant capital, Koforidua, to its lush landscapes and traditional festivals, visitors can explore historic towns, hike through dense forests, and immerse themselves in local culture. With attractions like the Boti Falls, Aburi Botanical Gardens, and the scenic Lake Volta, the Eastern Region promises unforgettable experiences for travelers seeking an authentic Ghanaian adventure.

Discover

Lush rainforests

The Eastern Region of Ghana is home to lush rainforests teeming with diverse plant and animal life. These forests provide vital habitats, regulate local climate, and offer serene environments for visitors to explore nature's beauty.

Akosombo dam

The Akosombo Dam, situated on the Volta River in southeastern Ghana, is a significant hydroelectric facility. Completed in 1965, it created Lake Volta, one of the world's largest artificial reservoirs. The dam generates electricity crucial for Ghana's power supply and supports various industries. Additionally, it facilitates irrigation, fishing, and transportation, contributing to the region's development and economic growth.

Aburi Botanical Gardens

The Aburi Botanical Garden, located in the Eastern Region of Ghana, is a renowned horticultural attraction. Established in 1890, it spans over 160 acres and showcases a diverse collection of tropical plants and trees. Visitors can explore lush gardens, picturesque landscapes, and scenic trails while enjoying the tranquility of nature. The garden serves as a recreational space, educational center, and research facility, making it a popular destination for locals and tourists alike.

Fun in Kwahu

Kwahu, nestled in the Eastern Region of Ghana, is renowned for its scenic beauty and vibrant cultural heritage. Perched atop the Kwahu Plateau, it offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. Kwahu is famous for its annual Easter festivities, drawing crowds from far and wide to partake in paragliding, street parties, and cultural celebrations. Additionally, the region boasts diverse outdoor activities, historical sites, and traditional markets, making it a must-visit destination for travelers seeking adventure and cultural immersion.

Kente

Kente weaving, a cherished tradition in Ghana's Eastern Region, involves intricate hand-weaving of vibrant fabric strips into meaningful patterns. Symbolizing wealth and identity, kente cloth is deeply rooted in Ghanaian culture, worn proudly on special occasions and ceremonies.

Adventure

ATV adventures in Aburi offer an exciting way to explore the scenic landscapes of Ghana's Eastern Region. Riders navigate rugged trails and hilly terrain, enjoying panoramic views and adrenaline-pumping experiences. Organized by local operators, these tours provide well-maintained vehicles and experienced guides for a safe and memorable adventure.

Historical Landmarks

Explore the rich history of the Eastern Region by visiting its historical landmarks, each telling a unique story of the region's past.
